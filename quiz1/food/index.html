Soto Betawi

Soto Betawi is a type of soup that originated from the Betawi people of Jakarta. It is made with beef, offal, coconut milk, and various spices such as galangal, lemongrass, and candlenut. It is usually served with rice, emping (melinjo crackers), sambal (chili sauce), and pickles. Soto Betawi is a hearty and flavorful dish that can warm you up on a cold day.

Kerak Telor

Kerak Telor is a traditional snack that is also from the Betawi culture. It is made by frying glutinous rice with eggs, dried shrimp, coconut, and shallots on a charcoal fire. The result is a crispy and savory pancake that is topped with fried shallots and spicy coconut flakes. Kerak Telor is often sold by street vendors during festivals and celebrations in Jakarta.

Nasi Uduk

Nasi Uduk is another rice dish that is popular in Jakarta and other parts of Indonesia. It is rice cooked in coconut milk with lemongrass, pandan leaves, cloves, cinnamon, and other spices. Nasi Uduk has a rich and fragrant aroma and taste, and it can be eaten with various side dishes such as fried chicken, tofu, tempeh, eggs, vegetables, and sambal. Nasi Uduk is a simple but satisfying meal that can be enjoyed anytime of the day.

Gado-Gado

Gado-Gado is an Indonesian salad that consists of raw or blanched vegetables, boiled eggs, potatoes, tofu, tempeh, and lontong (rice cake) tossed in a peanut sauce dressing. Gado-Gado is a healthy and versatile dish that can be customized with different ingredients according to one's preference. Gado-Gado is also one of the national dishes of Indonesia, along with soto, satay, nasi goreng, and rendang.
